Business Transformation

Business transformation is the fundamental change to the way a business operates, whether that be moving into a new market or operating in a new way. It is an approach that attempts to align an organization’s activities relating to people, process and technology more closely with its business strategy and vision.

People - often called “Change Management”
Process - the identification and ordering of tasks and resources
Technology - the system, equipment, etc.

Examples of our business transformation work:

  • Large system implementation – Led the Organization and Change Management effort for an SAP implementation for the Supply Chain business in Johnson and Johnson’s Medical Devices and Diagnostics sector. Focused on areas such as: organization design, roles and responsibilities, stakeholder management, communications, change impact identification/ mitigation, and training. Also facilitated the team effectiveness of the overall project team responsible for the implementation. Results: engaged 1200 end users in a successful go live.
  • Process improvement for business execution – Consulted with the Leadership Team at Honeywell’s Space Systems Division on a key issue of delivering products on time to their customers. We transformed the way they viewed their operations via an approach that engaged the entire 1400 person population. This was done with a focus on process improvements in every department, a scorecard that gave constant feedback on progress, support for teams to make it happen, and a recognition plan with celebrations at critical milestones. Results: on time delivery went from 48% to 100%.
  • Workforce planning and downsizing – The acquisition of the once computer giant, Digital Equipment Corporation (DEC), to Compaq and eventually HP, was executed with sensitivity to their loyal employee base. We took on the serious work of consulting with leaders on their resource needs and then training them on how to manage their teams through the transition. Dealing with the emotional side of change was a critical skillset that was taught. Results: the company went from 120,000 employees to 60,000 and then even further. Many continue to have successful careers within HP. To this day, you can find a former DEC employee who speaks with fondness about their experience working for the company.
