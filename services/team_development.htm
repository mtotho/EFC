Team Development

The need for groups of people to work effectively together as a team is an area of focus for many companies. It is one of the most widely-used methods of meeting deliverables; however, it is not without issues. Human beings are complex and need structure to come together and become high performing. Team development is a process and it needs to be managed as such to achieve sustainable results.

Developing high performing teams is a specialty at Eileen Flynn Consulting, LLC. We have worked with hundreds of teams using a research-based approach. It all starts with a focus on the fundamentals.

Examples of our team development work:

  • Cross-functional Global Team Development – Launched over 20 cross-functional drug development teams within Johnson and Johnson’s Pharmaceutical R&D organization. We utilized the team fundamentals and chartered each team. We then branched out into Latin America, EMEA and Asia Pacific to connect their teams with the US-based team. We paid specific attention to the differences in working with Japan, and formed a project team to build effective working relationships across the globe. Results: a common language and process was established for all drug development teams that accelerated deliverables.
  • Leadership Team Development – Consulted with Johnson and Johnson’s World-wide Safety and Industrial Hygiene Leadership Team on their strategy and tactics for implementing S&IH around the world. Designed and facilitated off-sites in every region to move this team towards high performance. Results: improved performance by 32% in one year based on team survey metrics.
  • Team Development Consortium – Established a consortium of HR employees within Johnson and Johnson to teach skills in developing teams. Created a “train-the-trainer” approach to include a toolkit and real-time shadowing to learn how to work with teams. Results: Built team development capability within the organization which allowed for a broader coverage of teams.
