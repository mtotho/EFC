Employee Engagement

The topic of employee engagement is a natural fit with our other topics of business transformation, team development and coaching. Keeping employees engaged is even more important during times of change, and teams are more effective when individual members are engaged.

Several years ago we were part of a research team that did an in-depth study on employee engagement. We found two organizations, Gallup and The Corporate Leadership Council, who had good data and interesting models for viewing this topic. The facts are clear, employee engagement is directly correlated to productivity and it impacts a company’s bottom line.

From our work, we came up with the following definition of employee engagement:

Engagement is the degree to which employees are satisfied with their jobs, feel valued, and experience collaboration and trust. Engaged employees will stay with the company longer, and continually find smarter, more effective ways to add value to the organization. The end result is a high-performing company where people are flourishing and productivity is increased and sustained.

Examples of our employee engagement work:

  • Engagement strategy development and leadership training – Designed and developed an engagement strategy within Johnson and Johnson’s Pharmaceutical Research and Development sector and educated all leaders on how to execute against it. The focus was on two critical roles that leaders must play to engage employees: 1. Connecting Employees with the Organization, 2. Guiding Employees’ Work and Performance. We created a menu of tools for leaders to utilize and tracked progress via action planning. Results: Improved scores in the employee engagement index of a follow-up employee morale survey.
  • Non-monetary rewards and recognition program – Designed a peer-to-peer recognition program within several organizations. This included the mechanics on delivery and also training on the “Effective Use of Positive Reinforcement.” Results: In all cases, program utilization was very high and employee engagement survey results improved.
  • Recognition plan for process improvement initiative – We described this initiative in our business transformation section: Honeywell’s Space Systems Division key issue of delivering products on time to their customers. The recognition plan is worth further elaboration as it relates to employee engagement. We facilitated the execution of an innovative themed recognition program that included planned celebrations for each critical milestone. Results: Published as a best practice in Performance Management magazine and recognized in process excellence audit.
